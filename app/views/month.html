1. Introduction

These statistics cover applications made through Apply for teacher training, the service that most people use to apply for initial teacher training courses in England.

Applications made in other ways such as directly to the teacher training provider, or through Teach First or UCAS undergraduate teacher training, are not included.

Teacher training candidates can apply for up to 3 courses in their first application. If their application does not lead to a place, they can apply again as many times as they like, for a single course at a time.

2. By status

A candidate's application can be in different states, including:

  • draft
  • submitted and awaiting provider decision
  • offer made by the provider (there are normally conditions to an offer)
  • offer accepted by candidate
  • offer declined by candidate
  • recruited (once any conditions are met)
  • application withdrawn (this can be done at any time after an application is submitted, even after accepting an offer)

This table counts only one application per candidate, rather than counting every application a candidate has in progress when the data was taken.

If they were making multiple applications, only their most "progressed" application is counted. The order in which the statuses show on the table is the order in which we're defining "progress".

For example, if a candidate has received an offer but is also waiting for a provider decision and has declined an offer, only their 'offer' application is counted.

When applying again candidates can only apply to one course at a time, so whichever application was in progress at the time the data was collected is counted in the apply again column, and none of their previous applications are counted.
